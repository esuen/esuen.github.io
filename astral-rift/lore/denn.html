SOLA DENN

Meridian Archivist, Second Class. Fourteen cross-referencing requests filed. Zero approved.

Personnel

She keeps asking.

Sola Denn has held the position of Meridian Archivist, Second Class, for nineteen years. In that time she has published eight papers, all suppressed. She has filed fourteen requests for cross-departmental data access, all denied. She has submitted three formal appeals to the Compact Review Board. All three are listed as pending. The oldest has been pending for seven years.

The Review Board adjourned sine die — indefinitely, without setting a date to reconvene — on the matter of cross-departmental data standards. Introduced in year 22. Adjourned sine die eleven times. Pending is not a status. It is a habitat.

Her personnel file contains two performance reviews. Both rate her work as "exemplary." Both recommend against promotion. The stated reason in both cases is identical: "Scope of inquiry exceeds role parameters."

Denn's office is on the fourth floor of the Meridian Archive, Room 412. It has not been reassigned in nineteen years. Reassignment requests are processed on average within three months.

Publication Record

Eight papers. All suppressed.

Every paper follows the same pattern. Denn identifies a data point that doesn't fit the Compact's official record. She documents it with the precision the Compact trained her to use. She submits it through proper channels. The paper is accepted, peer-reviewed, and suppressed before publication.

Suppressed "Inherited Navigation Behaviors in Post-Collapse Populations" — 847 pilot superstitions, 23% matching sealed protocols, 6% appearing across factions in languages the speakers did not share.
Suppressed "Settlement Chronology Inconsistencies in the Meridian Census" — Post-Collapse settlements appearing fully formed within one generation. No construction phases documented.
Suppressed "Autonomous Template Modification in Choir Production Systems" — Neural trees altering ship designs without external input. Cross-referenced with Compact Science Division file on Site 12.
Suppressed "Carbon Isotope Anomalies in Lattice Hull Fragments" — Dating discrepancies suggesting Lattice technology predates all other factions by centuries.
Suppressed "Foundry Assembly Complex TH: Unscheduled Template Deviation" — Production of hull incorporating cross-faction materials. No incoming transmission logged.
Suppressed "Spectroscopic Null Returns on Object 4-Null: Implications for Material Classification" — The hull that returns no reading. Implications for what "material" means.
Suppressed "Weave Anchor Point Geometry and Orb-Weaver Structural Analysis" — Dimensional filament brackets matching biological web construction at 400x scale.
Suppressed "Resonance Binding: The Missing Author" — All 4,200 pages of pre-Collapse documentation cite a foundational work by an author who appears in no other archive. Anywhere.

Each paper was accepted on merit. Each was suppressed after acceptance. The reviewers' notes survive alongside gaps where the suppression orders should be.

Access Requests

She sees the connections.

Denn's fourteen cross-referencing requests share a common structure. Each asks for read access to files held in a different Compact department. Each cites specific data points from her own research that correspond to data points in the target department's records. Each is denied on jurisdictional grounds.

The Compact organizes its archives by faction. Foundry records are in one department. Choir records in another. Bloom monitoring in a third. Denn's requests ask to read across faction boundaries. The Compact's filing system is not designed for this. Denn believes this is not an accident.

Denied Cross-reference: Choir neural tree modification logs × Foundry Complex TH production deviation.
Denied Cross-reference: Bloom behavioral evolution data × MTAD neural simulation outputs.
Denied Cross-reference: Lattice carbon dating anomalies × pre-Collapse archaeological timeline.
Denied Cross-reference: Object 4-Null spectroscopic data × Weave filament decay measurements.
Pending Cross-reference: All suppressed Denn publications × Compact Science Division classified index. Filed 4 years ago.

The five listed above are representative. The remaining nine follow the same pattern. Each connects two faction-specific datasets that the Compact stores separately. Each implies that the separation is the problem.

The Compact archives contain 4.3 million indexed documents across nineteen departments. Each department maintains its own classification system, its own access protocols, its own review board. A Foundry engineering report and a Choir biological survey can describe the same phenomenon in the same corridor on the same day and never appear in the same search result. Denn's requests ask to open a door between two rooms that were never connected. The distinction between security and architecture matters. Security can be justified. Architecture is just a choice someone made.

Compact Records Office, internal audit, year 38 post-Collapse. Distribution: limited.
Inference

The question she won't stop asking.

Denn has never stated her conclusion. Her papers present data. Her requests ask for more data. She has not published a synthesis, a theory, or a speculation. Every document in her file is a question, not an answer.

But the questions form a shape. Production systems modifying themselves across Choir and Foundry lines. Lattice minerals older than everything around them. A hull that returns no reading and brackets arranged like biology. Settlements that appeared complete, pilot superstitions in languages nobody taught them, and a foundational author who doesn't exist.

Each question, alone, is a curiosity. Together, they outline something the Compact's filing system was built to prevent anyone from seeing.
