Astral Rift

Your health is your ammo. Real-time multiplayer space combat where every shot makes you weaker and every decision could be your last.

Unreal Engine 5 C++ HLSL Python JAX Niagara VFX Gameplay Ability System
Top-down multiplayer · 1–8 players + AI · 10-minute matches · In development

What it looks like

King of the Hill — three teams fight for control as the zone rotates between a black hole that warps projectiles, nebula clouds with fog-of-war, and open corridors.

The core tension

Your energy is everything. Health, ammo, and abilities all draw from the same pool. Fire a bullet and you lose health. Activate overdrive and you drain faster. Use a bomb and you might not survive the return fire.

This creates a decision loop that never stops: attack now and risk dying, or hold back and let your opponent grow stronger. There's no hiding behind a full health bar—if you're not spending energy, you're not fighting.

How it plays

Pick your ship

Eight ships with mode-specific ability loadouts. The Specter goes invisible for stealth ambushes. The Titan fires wall-piercing bombs and locks down chokepoints with Fortify. The Bastion deploys auto-targeting sentry turrets and barrier walls in FFA, or carries allies as turrets in team modes. The Comet rockets in at double speed, drops shrapnel bombs, and escapes before you can react. Every ship plays differently in FFA vs team modes.

The arena fights back

Black holes warp your projectiles and teleport ships that get too close. Nebula clouds create fog-of-war where you can only see what's nearby. Asteroids provide cover—until someone blows them up.

AI that learns

Neural network bots trained through billions of simulated combat steps learn to fight from scratch—no hand-coded strategies. They discover bullet timing, bomb placement, mine traps, and energy management through iterative reward shaping. Hand-tuned bots add variety with personality-driven behaviors: setting ambushes, retreating into nebula clouds, and coordinating team focus fire. Eight ship classes, each with distinct tactics.

Risk and reward

Kill streaks increase your bounty, making you worth more points—but also a bigger target. Pick up items to gain charges for devastating abilities like MIRV missiles and repulsor blasts. Every advantage paints a target on your back.

Free-For-All Deathmatch

Every ship for itself. Green pickups scattered across nebula clouds and corridor intersections grant random ability charges—MIRV missiles, repulsor blasts, ripper fields. Ship-specific pools mean a Tempest gets burst charges where a Viper gets ripper. Mode-specific item spawn zones create contested hotspots that reward map control.

King of the Hill

Three teams fight over a rotating capture zone. Hold it to score—first to 100 wins. The zone moves between five locations: an open cross, a black hole, a nebula cloud, and two outposts. Each location changes how you fight.

Capture the Flag

Two teams across a corridor-filled arena with procedurally varied layouts. Steal the enemy flag, navigate chokepoints back to your base. Your flag must be home to capture—so defense matters as much as offense. Bots autonomously assign roles: fast ships grab flags, escorts protect the carrier, defenders guard the base.

Rift Runners (single-player)

A roguelike mode with procedurally generated arenas, escalating enemy waves, and build-defining upgrade choices between each rift. Pick from 13+ upgrades across four tiers—stack damage for a glass cannon run, or build tanky and outlast everything. Ten rifts, two bosses, twenty minutes per run.

The backstory

In the early 2000s, Sony Online Entertainment released Cosmic Rift—a top-down spaceship combat game where your health and your ammo were the same resource. Every shot you fired made you more vulnerable. That tension—spend energy to kill, or save it to survive—produced some of the most compelling competitive gameplay I've experienced. The servers shut down years ago, and nothing replaced it.

Astral Rift is my attempt to bring that feeling back. Same core tension, modern engine, new mechanics.

Under the hood

Built entirely in C++ on Unreal Engine 5.7—no Blueprints, no visual scripting.

AI combat system

Two parallel AI systems. Hand-tuned bots use a four-layer architecture: goal stack, utility-based state machine, Reynolds steering, and predictive weapons—with a genetic algorithm evolving 36 parameters per ship class. Neural network bots train via PPO reinforcement learning in a pure JAX physics simulation across 8,192 parallel environments. The latest generation achieves a 78% win rate against hand-tuned opponents after 500M training steps. Iterative self-play produces increasingly strong opponents. 61 automated headless behavior tests verify every game mechanic in 6 seconds. Trained policies deploy via ONNX with automated eval-before-promotion.

Custom shaders

Black holes with Schwarzschild-inspired spacetime distortion and Keplerian particle orbits. Nebula clouds with Kolmogorov turbulence. Procedural asteroid surfaces with Voronoi crater patterns.

Performance at scale

Projectile object pooling with zero combat allocations, centralized actor registry, single-pass HUD rendering, tick budgeting, and material instance reuse—designed for 50+ concurrent players.

Complete systems

FFA Deathmatch, KOTH, CTF, and Duel match flow, mode-specific ability loadout system, per-mode item spawn zones, team assignment, kill feed, HUD with minimap and flag status, ship selection with sprite previews, key rebinding, bounty scoring, data-driven map editor with procedural layout generation, auto-follow spectator camera, and networking foundation with server-authoritative movement and replicated ship state. 199 total tests: 61 automated headless behavior tests, 30 JAX sim cross-checks, 34 networking tests, and 74 visual verification tests—with a 25-scenario auto-playing visual test mode for rapid QA.
